Maison · No. I

A house of quiet luxury, tailored by hand in London.

Skins of provenance.

Every pelt arrives at the atelier traceable to its origin — Russian sable, Scandinavian mink, North American silver fox — selected by hand for length, sheen and the sound it makes when stroked against the grain.

Origin · 01

Cut on a London block.

Patterns are drafted to the body, not the bolt. Each garment passes through twelve hands before it leaves Bruton Place — drawn, dressed, lined, stitched and pressed with the patience of an older century.

Atelier · 02

A wardrobe meant to stay.

FURĒM is not seasonal. It is the coat your daughter will wear on a Tuesday in 2049, the jacket that survives a thousand opera nights and a hundred late dinners — restored, never replaced.

The Atelier

A craft measured
in hours,
not minutes.

Fur is the slowest material in fashion. A single jacket asks for one hundred and eighty hours of considered work — knife to pattern, pelt to lining — performed in daylight, on a London block, by hands that have done it before.

    Average bench time, from drafting to final press.

  • 12

  • 1949

    The year the bench we still use was first set.

  • ∞

    Restorations covered, for as long as the coat exists.

Editorial · I

A walk across Westminster.

Photographed in the rain, before the bridge filled with traffic.

Our winter campaign begins where most London stories do — at the foot of the clock tower, with cold air, wet pavement, and a coat heavy enough to be felt on the shoulders. The result is a quiet sort of cinema.

Editorial · II

After hours on Regent.

A black coat. A small bag. A long evening.

FURĒM is built for the hours after the city has gone home — for theatre exits, late dinners and quiet drives north along the Mall. The pieces are weighted to feel honest in cold air, and lined to feel forgiving in warm rooms.
